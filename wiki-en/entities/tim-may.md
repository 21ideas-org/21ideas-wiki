In the 21ideas sources, **Timothy C. May (Tim May)** is a key early [[en/entities/cypherpunks|cypherpunk]] figure whose "Crypto Anarchist Manifesto" articulates a vision of cryptography enabling anonymous interaction, markets, and contracts outside state control — a worldview that later strongly overlaps with the motivation narratives around [[en/concepts/bitcoin|Bitcoin]].

## The Crypto Anarchist Manifesto (core ideas)

From the [Crypto Anarchist Manifesto](https://21ideas.org/manifest-kriptoanarhista), the manifesto argues that:

- computer networks + cryptography will enable **anonymous communication and commerce**;
- reputation becomes central when real-world identity is removed;
- states will attempt to slow or ban such tools, but the technology's spread is difficult to stop;
- cryptographic protocols shift what can be regulated and what can be [[en/concepts/censorship-resistance|censored]].

## Relationship to the cypherpunk movement

The source frames the manifesto as a document shared with the cypherpunk community and tied to early in-person gatherings ("physical cypherpunks"). In 21ideas' broader narrative, this movement is the intellectual and cultural soil from which Bitcoin later emerges.

See also [[en/entities/cypherpunks|cypherpunks]] and the "cypherpunks write code" ethos.
