In the 21ideas sources, **Phil Zimmermann** is primarily referenced as the creator of **PGP (Pretty Good Privacy)**, a foundational tool for encryption and digital signatures — and as a symbol of the [[en/entities/cypherpunks|cypherpunk]]-era fight to make strong cryptography widely usable.

## PGP as a practical privacy tool

The [21ideas PGP practice guide](https://21ideas.org/pgp-verify) defines:

- **PGP** as a program for encryption and digital signatures, created by Zimmermann (1991),
- **OpenPGP** as an open standard created later (1997),
- and explains why signature verification matters for software authenticity.

In the [[en/concepts/bitcoin|Bitcoin]] context, this mindset maps to "don't trust, verify" applied to software distribution and release integrity.

## Cypherpunk-era context (export controls)

[[en/series/genesis-files|Genesis Files Part II]] notes that PGP was treated under US export rules as "munitions," and it references cypherpunk activism around that era (including symbolic actions like "crypto" shirts). In the 21ideas narrative, this is part of the broader struggle to make [[en/concepts/privacy|privacy]] tools unstoppable.
